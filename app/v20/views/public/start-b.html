! Warning You can be fined up to £250,000 and/or sent to prison if you sell, hire or buy an item containing ivory if it has not been registered or certified.

Before you sell or hire an item containing ivory, you must use this service to:

Either Register the item for a single sale or for hiring out

It costs £XX to register an item if it's one of the following:

  • a musical instrument made before 1975 with less than 20% ivory
  • an item made before 1947 with less than 10% ivory
  • a portrait miniature made before 1918
  • an item you’ll sell or hire to an approved museum

Or Apply for a certificate for the item

It costs £XXX to apply for a certificate. Your application will be checked by an approved assessor. It may take up to 30 days.

The item must have been made before 1918 and be the rareset and most important of its type.

Read the guide to selling, hiring and buying items with ivory.

You'll need:

  • a photo of the item
  • a debit or credit card
