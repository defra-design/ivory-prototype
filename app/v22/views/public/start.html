! Warning You can be fined up to £250,000 and/or sent to prison if you buy, sell or hire an ivory item that has not been registered or certified.

Use this service to:

  • register an ivory item
  • apply for a certificate for an ivory item

It costs £## to register or £### to apply for a certificate.

You do not have to register and apply for a certificate for the same item. An item would either need registration or a certificate.

Read the guide to selling, hiring and buying items with ivory.

You'll need:

  • a photo of the item
  • a debit or credit card
Start now

Before you start

You can register:

  • a musical instrument made before 1975 with less than 20% ivory
  • an item made before 1947 with less than 10% ivory
  • a portrait miniature made before 1918
  • an item you’ll sell or hire to an approved museum

You can apply for a certificate if you can prove the item was made before 1918 and has outstanding artistic, cultural or historical value.

Other ways to apply

You can also register or apply for a certificate using a paper form.
