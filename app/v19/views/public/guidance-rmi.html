Guidance

Certifying ivory items of outstandingly high artistic, cultural or historical value

How to apply for an exemption certificate, keep it up to date, request a replacement, or appeal a decision if your application has been rejected.

Published 1 October 2021
From:
Animal and Plant Health Agency

Get an exemption certificate for items of outstandingly high value

If your item is of outstandingly high artistic, cultural or historical value, you’ll need to apply for an expert to certify the item.

What qualifies as ‘outstandingly high artistic, cultural or historical value’

Each item needs to be assessed individually by recognised experts. They set a very high threshold when approving items for sale or hire.

They’ll consider various things, such as whether an item:

  • is unique or extremely rare
  • is of high artistic or aesthetic quality, for example from a known artist, school or studio
  • is in better condition than other items like it
  • is part of a well-known collection
  • has an important history
  • is important to a specific place or region
  • has previously been recognised for its value or status, for example being recognised as a national treasure

How the certification process works

You must apply online for a certificate. There is an administration fee of £250, which you must pay even if your item is not approved by an expert.

After you have submitted your application, you must wait to receive your exemption certificate before you can sell or hire out the item. This usually takes 30 days.

Apply for an exemption certificate.

Appeal a rejection decision

If you disagree with our decision to reject your application for an exemption certificate, you can appeal it.

For an appeal to be successful, you’ll most likely have to provide new evidence that your item meets the threshold of having outstandingly high artistic, cultural or historical value.

To start an appeal, contact the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA) at ivory@apha.gov.uk.

Selling or hiring out an item that already has an exemption certificate

You need to tell us if you are selling or hiring out an item that already has an exemption certificate, so that we can keep our records up to date.

Contact the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA) at ivory@apha.gov.uk.

You’ll need:

  • a copy of the certificate
  • a credit or debit card to pay a £xx admin fee

Keeping an exemption certificate up to date

You must let us know if you discover something about the item that means it may no longer be exempt.

Contact the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA) at ivory@apha.gov.uk.

Request a replacement exemption certificate

You can request a replacement exemption certificate if you’ve:

  • lost it or
  • bought an exempt item and are unable to get the certificate from the seller

Contact the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA) at ivory@apha.gov.uk.
