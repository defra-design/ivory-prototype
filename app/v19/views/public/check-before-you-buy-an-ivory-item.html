Guidance

Check before you buy an ivory item

Check if an item you want to buy has been registered or has a certificate.

Published 2 December 2019
From:
Animal and Plant Health Agency

Your legal responsibility

It is illegal to buy an ivory item that does not have a valid registration number or certificate.

Before buying or offering to buy an ivory item, you’re legally responsible for checking that it:

  • is not included in the ivory ban
  • has a valid registration number or certificate

If you’re buying an item a seller has registered, the registration is only valid for that single transaction. If you want to sell the item, you must register it again and pay the fee.

If you’re buying an item that has a certificate, you should collect the certificate from the seller after you’ve paid. You need this certificate (or a replacement) to sell the item in the future.

Check an item

To check an item you should:

  • put the item’s registration or certificate number into our online system to see if it’s genuine
  • make sure the seller’s document matches the item
