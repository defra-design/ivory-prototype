The ivory must be integral to the item. This means either:

  • you cannot remove the ivory easily or without risk of damage, for example a large piece of furniture with a small piece of ivory inlay
  • the ivory is essential to the design or function of the item, for example a detachable ivory knob on a teapot
How do I know if the item was made before 1947?

You can use:

  • an original receipt or bill
  • a date mark on the item
  • written proof from an expert, such as an antiques specialist
  • personal knowledge or evidence that it’s been in the family since 1947 or before
  • an article or a catalogue published before 1 January 1947 with photos or a detailed description of the item
  • carbon dating
How can I tell if the item has less than 10% ivory?

You must give a reasonable assessment of the volume of ivory in your item. In some cases, it’s easy to do this by eye. In others, you’ll need to take measurements.

Do not include any empty spaces, for instance the space within a chest of drawers or a teapot.

If it’s difficult to calculate ivory volume without damaging the item, you can make an assessment based on knowledge of similar items.
